Our Work

Community-driven projects, institution-level partnerships.

Our projects are designed to address pressing social, economic, educational, and policy challenges through evidence-based and culturally relevant collaboration.

Public Policy & Fiscal Transparency

Enhancing Fiscal Transparency is a partnership with the University of Connecticut and the U.S. Treasury Department to improve budget transparency and public engagement in Uzbekistan.

  • Public finance
  • Institutional development
  • Governance

Domestic Violence Prevention

Vatandosh Connect supports implementation of the Plumas model for rehabilitating domestic violence offenders. The project works with local social protection institutions to address harmful norms and build non-violent family and community practices.

  • Social reform
  • Family wellbeing
  • Local capacity

Educational Projects

We support educational opportunities and institution-building, including work toward an American-model engineering university in collaboration with the Colorado School of Mines.

  • Engineering education
  • Workforce development
  • Higher education

Children's Rights in Uzbekistan

Collaborative initiatives support child welfare in Uzbekistan through research, social services, and institutional partnerships focused on vulnerable children and families.

  • Child welfare
  • Social adaptation
  • Policy support

Poverty Amelioration

Through the Poverty Stoplight Methodology and partnerships with RCSAD Tashkent and Ferghana branches, Vatandosh Connect supports entrepreneurship training, financial literacy, and community-driven pathways out of poverty.

  • Poverty Stoplight
  • Entrepreneurship
  • Financial literacy

Building a Future for Children with Autism

In collaboration with UNC TEACCH and RCSAD Qarshi, this initiative strengthens autism services in Uzbekistan by training specialists, introducing evidence-based diagnostic methods, and improving early detection.

  • Autism diagnostics
  • UNC TEACCH
  • Professional training

Animal Protection: Terra Vita Partnership

Vatandosh Connect supports the Terra Vita shelter in Samarkand, helping rescue, care for, and rehome abandoned animals while promoting humane treatment and community awareness.

  • Animal protection
  • Community awareness
  • Samarkand

Cultural & Scientific Research

The organization supports research, cultural exchange, and public education that celebrate Uzbekistan's history while connecting local institutions with global expertise.

  • Cultural heritage
  • Research
  • Exchange

Partner model

From need to implementation

A clearer website can show partners exactly how Vatandosh works: identify need, build coalition, secure resources, implement locally, and share lessons publicly.

1. Identify

Work with local partners to define the problem and implementation context.

2. Connect

Bring diaspora professionals, universities, agencies, funders, and civil society into the same project frame.

3. Deliver

Support training, coordination, reporting, and storytelling so the work can grow.
