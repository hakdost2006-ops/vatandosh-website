Vatandosh Terra Vita Support Fund

Help feed and care for homeless dogs and cats in Samarkand

In Uzbekistan, many abandoned animals survive without reliable food, shelter, sterilization, or veterinary care. Terra Vita gives dogs and cats in Samarkand a safer place while they wait for treatment, adoption, or long-term care.

Why Americans may not know this

Animal welfare looks different in Uzbekistan.

Many U.S. donors are used to county shelters, animal control, local foundations, and large veterinary networks. In Uzbekistan, shelter capacity is limited, many rescue efforts are volunteer-led, and affordable sterilization or emergency veterinary care can be difficult to access.

What your gift supports

Food, medicine, veterinary care, sterilization support, rescue transport, shelter supplies, adoption outreach, and public education around responsible pet ownership.

Street animals

Free-roaming dogs and cats often rely on volunteers for food, treatment, and rescue.

Limited shelters

There is no comprehensive national shelter network; local groups carry much of the burden.

Vet care gaps

Sterilization, vaccination, and emergency care remain expensive or unevenly available.

Growing awareness

Public concern is rising, especially among younger people and volunteer networks.
