==== PROJECTS TEXT ====

Our Projects
At Vatandosh Connect, our projects are designed to drive meaningful, sustainable change in Uzbekistan by addressing the most pressing social, economic, and educational challenges. Each initiative is carefully developed in collaboration with strategic partners, ensuring that solutions are evidence-based, culturally relevant, and community-driven. We focus on capacity building, policy reform, and grassroots engagement, empowering individuals and institutions to take an active role in shaping a better future for Uzbekistan. By fostering innovation, education, and social inclusion, we create impactful projects that leave a lasting legacy
Public Policy
Enhancing Fiscal Transparency – A partnership with UCONN and the U.S. Treasury Department to improve budget transparency and public engagement.
Domestic Violence Prevention
Implementation of the Plumas model for rehabilitating domestic violence offenders.
Educational Projects
Developing an American-model engineering university in collaboration with the Colorado School of Mines.
Children's Rights in Uzbekistan
Collaborative initiatives to support child welfare in Uzbekistan
Poverty Amelioration
Vatandosh Connect is tackling poverty in Uzbekistan through data-driven solutions like the "Poverty Stoplight Methodology", empowering families to assess and overcome financial, educational, and social challenges. By partnering with RCSAD Tashkent and Ferghana branches, we provide entrepreneurship training, financial literacy programs, and community-driven interventions** to create sustainable pathways out of poverty.
Building a Future for Children with Autism
Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a complex neurodevelopmental condition affecting communication, social interaction, and behavior. While early diagnosis and intervention are crucial for improving outcomes, Uzbekistan has historically faced significant barriers in autism detection due to a lack of standardized diagnostic tools and trained professionals.
Recognizing this challenge, Vatandosh Connect, in collaboration with UNC TEACCH (University of North Carolina - Chapel Hill) and the Republican Center for Social Adaptation of Children (RCSAD) Qarshi branch, launched an initiative to enhance autism services in Uzbekistan. This groundbreaking project aimed to train specialists, introduce evidence-based diagnostic methods, and improve early autism detection across the country.
